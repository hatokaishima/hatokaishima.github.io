a Chinese translation of my code
26 May 2024

Sometimes I am confused about what Chinese my code, ‘to increase the happiness of mankind and make a brave new world’, I should translate into. Literally, it can be interpreted word by word as ‘增加人類幸福，建造美麗新世界’. However, it is not of such literary taste. And what I want to convey are more than that.

According to Oxford Advanced Learner’s Dictionary (OALD), increase means:

to make something greater in amount, number, value, etc.

In the literal sense, its Chinese is ‘增加’. But the word in my code means enhance, whose explanation in OALD is:

to increase or further improve the good quality, value or status of somebody/something

Exactly, it means ‘further enhancing the increasing of something’. Or, we can say ‘增進’ in Chinese.

‘happiness’ here is not just joy (‘快樂’ in Chinese) as in Bach’s melody of Jesu, Joy of Man’s Desiring or like Bertrand Russel’s The Conquest of Happiness, but welfare, which is:

the general health, happiness and safety of a person, an animal or a group

It means ‘福祉’.

‘mankind’ is:

all humans, thought about as one large group; the human race

Surely it is ‘人類’ in Chinese, but we have a literary alternative–’蒼生’.

Some may think ‘make a brave new world’ is just like ‘make a cake’.

to create or prepare something by combining materials or putting parts together

It is not the case. ‘make’ here means:

to cause something to be a success

or, to be more precise,

to manage to reach or go to a place or position

Probably we can say ‘鑄就’, since it requires effort and is not a piece of cake.

‘a brave new world’ refers to Aldous Huxley’s book, as we know. ‘brave’ is not:

(of a person) willing to do things that are difficult, dangerous or painful; not afraid

but as part of the phrase ‘brave new’, which means:

new in an impressive way

Although ‘a brave new world’ is sometimes ironic as OALD says:

a situation or society that changes in a way that is meant to improve people’s lives but is often a source of extra problems

it is not my intention. I believe it is a different and better one from our present-day world. ‘world’ can be translate into ‘天下’ or ‘世界’ or ‘世間’, but in this phrase, we can perceive it as a whole and interpret it as ‘璀璨新天’, even though ‘璀璨’ in Chinese means ‘bright’ to some extent.

So I think my code may be translated into ‘增進蒼生福祉，鑄就璀璨新天’. I believe, however, there must be a better translation.
